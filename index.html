Popular Places to Visit

Below are few tourist places those were visited frequently by our tourists

Mysore

At a distance of ~150 Kms from Bangalore - Mysore is the land of Royal majesty and beautiful landscapes is one of the most popular tourist destinations in South India. The city is a hub for all the nature, wildlife and adventure lovers. Mysore Palace, Brindavan Gardens, KRS Dam, Mysore Zoo, St.Philomenas Church, Jaganmohan Palace, Lalita Mahal Palace, Karanji Lake are the main tourist places to visit in Mysore

 

Coorg

At a distance of ~248 kms from Bangalore - Coorg or Kodagu is a district in Karnataka and Madikeri is the district headquarters. It is also known as the Scotland of India and is the most famous places to visit in India and among popular 2 day trip near Bangalore Talacauvery, Abbey Falls, Dubare, Bylakuppe Golden Temple, Raja's Seat, Iruppu Falls are the main tourist places to visit in Coorg

 

Ooty

At a distance of ~343 Kms from Bangalore - Ooty is also known as Udhagamandalam and Ooty is often referred to as 'Queen of hill stations' The toy train, known as Nilgiri Mountain Railway that runs from Mettupalayam to Ooty is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must be experienced. Ooty Lake, Botanical Garden, Rose Garden, Wenlock Downs, Pine Forest, and Emerald Lake are the main tourist places to visit in Ooty

 

Wayanad

At a distance of ~278 Kms from Bangalore - Wayanad is one of the popular hill stations near Bangalore and one of the best 2 day trip from Bangalore Wayanad has a rich history and the hills of Wayanad have several evidences of the New Stone Age civilization. Wayanad is also famous for Tea and Coffee plantations Pookote Lake, Banasura sagar dam, Karappuzha Dam, Meenmutty Falls, Soochipara Falls, Kanthampara Falls are the main tourist places to visit in Wayanad

 

Munnar

At a distance of ~476 Kms from Bangalore - Munnar is a fascinating hill resort located in Idukki. Munnar is also known for Neelakurinji flowers, which blooms once in 12 years. The kurinji season in Munnar is a magnificent sight as the hills and valleys are blanketed by Neelakurinji blooms. Mattupetty Dam, Pothamedu Viewpoint, Tea Museum, Mattupetty Dam, Echo point, Kundala Lake, Top Station, Anamudi peak, Devikulam, are the main tourist places to visit in Munnar

 

Tirupati

At a distance of ~259 Kms from Bangalore - Tirupati is a pilgrimage city often referred as a synonym to Tirumala and called as Tirumala Tirupati, which is a sacred temple town famous for Sri Venkateswara Temple also known as Tirumala Tirupati Balaji Temple. Varahaswami temple, Tiruchanur temple of Goddess Padmavati, Govindaraja temple, Srinivasa Mangapuram, Avanakshamma temple, ISKCON Lord Krishna Temple are the main places to visit in Tirupati

 

Dandeli

At a distance of ~460 Kms from Bangalore - Dandeli wildlife Sanctuary with dense forests is a natural habitat for wildlife, including tigers, leopards, black panthers, elephants, gaur, deer, antelopes, and bears. The jungle is also home to several varieties of reptiles and almost 300 varieties of birds Syntheri Rocks, Ulavi temple, Sykes point, Supa hydroelectric dam, Anashi National Park, and Moulang are the popular tourist places to visit in Dandeli.

 

Murudeshwar

At a distance of ~510 Kms from Bangalore - Murudeshwar is another name of the Hindu god Shiva. A huge towering statue of Lord Shiva, visible from great distances, is the second tallest statue of Lord Shiva in the world. This temple is built on the Kanduka Hill which is surrounded on three sides by the waters of the Arabian Sea. Gokarna, Idagunji, Kollur, Maravanthe Beach, Udupi, Om beach are the nearby places to visit from Murudeshwar

 

Dharmasthala

At a distance of ~300 Kms from Bangalore - Dharmasthala is an Indian temple on the banks of the Nethravathi River. Lakshadeepa, the festival of lights, is the annual festival of Dharmasthala in November–December. The town is known for its Dharmasthala Temple which houses the shrine of Shiva, Manjunatha, Ammanavaru, Chandranath and the Dharma Daivas (guardian spirits of Dharma) — Kalarahu, Kalarkayi, Kumaraswamy and Kanyakumari

 

Vellore

At a distance of ~210 Kms from Bangalore - Vellore is the home of Christian Medical College & Hospital and Vellore Institute of Technology. Vellore region is the top exporter of finished leather goods in the country. Vellore leather accounts for more than 37% of the country's export of leather and leather-related products. Fort, Jalakanteswara Temple, Tipu Mahal, Golden Temple, Palamathi Hills, Vallimalai Subramanyar Temple, Mahadeva Malai Temple are the places to visit in Vellore

 

Kodaikanal

At a distance of ~465 Kms from Bangalore - Kodaikanal Located in the hills of Dindigul is a beautiful hill station, famous for its breathtaking landscapes and refreshing atmosphere. Kodaikanal translates to “The Gift of the Forest” which it undoubtedly is;bountiful forests it is dotted with are the proof.Pillar Rocks, Silver Cascade Falls, Dolphins Nose, Moir Point, Kurinji Temple, Subrahmanya Temple, La Salette Church, Christ the King Church, Lutheran Church are the places to visit

 

Chennai

At a distance of ~350 Kms from Bangalore - Chennai is a city that requires time and effort to really get to know and appreciate it. It's a city that requires you to explore below its surface and delve into its distinctive culture. Chennai's historic neighborhood of Mylapore is often referred to as the soul of the city. Marina beach, which is the longest urban beach in India, starts from near Fort Saint George and runs south for 13 kilometers. T. Nagar attracts hordes of shoppers hunting for everything from to saris to gold
